The Playbooks They Keep Using

Reading through months of RCMP reports, the same scripts keep appearing. Scammers are not creative — they're systematic. They test what works and repeat it. Here are the five plays hitting Oceanside hardest right now.

The Fake Bank Call. A caller claims to be from your bank's fraud department. They tell you your account has been compromised. They ask you to move money to a "safe" account or make e-transfers to verify your identity. Your real bank will never ask you to do this. Never.

The Cryptocurrency Social Media Ad. An ad appears on Facebook, Instagram, or a linked website promising extraordinary returns on a crypto investment. The platform looks legitimate. The testimonials look real. The money disappears. This scam alone has cost Parksville residents over $60,000 in 2026.

The Romance Scam. A stranger makes contact online. The relationship builds over weeks or months. Eventually, they need money — for an emergency, a medical bill, a flight to come see you. In April, one resident lost $12,000 this way. The emotional cost is harder to quantify.

The "Relative in Trouble" Scam. A call or text claiming to be from a grandchild, niece, nephew, or cousin. They're in trouble — arrested, in hospital, stranded. They need money immediately and ask you not to tell anyone. This scam has hit Oceanside residents repeatedly, including a $9,000 loss in November 2025.

The Remote Access Scam. A caller from a "tech company" or "credit card provider" tells you there's a problem with your computer or account. They ask you to download software so they can fix it remotely. Once in, they drain accounts, steal credentials, and disappear. One resident lost over $7,000 this way in April 2026.

Who Is Being Targeted

The pattern in RCMP reports suggests seniors are disproportionately targeted — the fake bank calls, the relative scams, and the romance scams all tend to exploit trust, urgency, and unfamiliarity with digital fraud. But the cryptocurrency scams are hitting younger residents too. No age group is immune.
